About Her Home

History of Afghan Catering

Afghanistan has a rich culinary tradition shaped by centuries of cultural influences from Central Asia, Persia, South Asia, and the Middle East. Traditional Afghan catering includes dishes like kabuli pulao, mantu, bolani, and a variety of breads and sweets. These dishes are not only a source of nourishment but also a reflection of Afghan hospitality, community, and celebration.

Women have historically played a central role in preparing these meals, using recipes passed down through generations. Today, many Afghan women are using their cooking skills to create catering businesses, turning their talent into a source of income and independence.

Handmade Crafts by Afghan Women

Afghan women are also skilled in creating handmade crafts such as carpets, textiles, embroidery, and jewelry. Each handcrafted item tells a story of heritage and artistry, reflecting the culture and history of Afghanistan. Carpet weaving, in particular, is an ancient tradition, with intricate patterns passed from mother to daughter.

Through their crafts, women are able to preserve cultural knowledge while generating income for themselves and their families. These handmade products are not only beautiful but also carry the spirit and resilience of their makers.

The Struggle and Empowerment of Afghan Women

Many Afghan women face significant challenges in earning a living due to limited opportunities, social restrictions, and ongoing political and economic instability. Despite these difficulties, they continue to use their skills and talents to support their families and communities.

By participating in initiatives like Her Home, Afghan women are empowered to share their talents with the world, gain financial independence, and create sustainable livelihoods. Supporting their work helps preserve Afghan culture, promote gender equality, and provide hope for future generations.
