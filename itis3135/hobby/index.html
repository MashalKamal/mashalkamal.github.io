Afghan Cooking at Home

I love cooking Afghan food for my family and friends during my free time. Cooking is not just a hobby for me — it is part of who I am and where I come from.

I grew up in an Afghan household where food was always being prepared. I learned by watching my family cook traditional meals like rice, kababs, and bread.

Who I Am

I am an Afghan student who enjoys cooking traditional food for my family and friends. Cooking is one of the ways I stay connected to my culture.

I learned cooking from my family, and it has always been part of my life growing up in an Afghan home.

What I Cook

I cook Afghan dishes like Kabuli Pulao, kababs, sweets, and traditional drinks.

These foods are very important in Afghan culture and are usually shared during family meals and celebrations.

When I Cook

I usually cook in my free time, especially on weekends or when guests come over.

Cooking becomes more special during holidays, celebrations, and family gatherings.

Where I Cook

I cook at home in my kitchen where I feel comfortable and connected to my culture.

Afghan food is made everywhere, but home cooking is the most meaningful to me.

How I Cook

I cook using traditional Afghan methods passed down in my family.

This includes grilling meat, cooking rice, and using spices for flavor.

Why I Cook

I cook Afghan food because it connects me to my culture, family history, and childhood memories.

In Afghan culture, food is a way of showing love, respect, and hospitality to others.

Cooking also makes me happy because I love sharing food with my family and friends and seeing them enjoy it.
